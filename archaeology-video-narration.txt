Archaeologists discovered a 3,000-year-old statue of an Amazonian goddess buried deep in the jungle. Found in a remote Ecuadorian site, it’s carved with intricate details of a civilization long thought to have no written history. But the statue’s metal inlays—uncommon in the region—defy explanation. Experts are baffled by its advanced craftsmanship. Why did this artifact exist here? How did it survive untouched for millennia? What secrets does it hold?
